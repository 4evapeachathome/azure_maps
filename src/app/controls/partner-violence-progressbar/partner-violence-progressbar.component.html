LEVEL 3

Level 3 abuse includes more severe actions such as the use of a weapon, choking, or attempting to strangulate. Different forms of violence include:

  • Intimate terrorism / patriarchal terrorism: where aggression is mostly from male to female with the goal of control and domination, instilling fear.
  • Violent resistance: occurs when a partner resists aggression, often in self-defense, and can lead to injury.
  • Situational couple violence / common couple violence: involves bidirectional, low-intensity aggression, often because of conflict rather than for control or self-defense.
