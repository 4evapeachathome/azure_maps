Physical Abuse

Physical abuse occurs when an individual uses force to harm their partner or attempts to exert control over them through physical violence. This can involve actions such as hitting, choking, grabbing, pinching, strangling, shoving, slapping, hair-pulling, biting, maiming, or killing, or threatening with weapons to inflict injury. Additionally, physical abuse may include behaviors like denying medical care, forcing substance use (such as drugs or alcohol), or isolating a partner from friends and family.
