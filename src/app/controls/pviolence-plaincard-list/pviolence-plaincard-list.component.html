What is Intimate Partner Violence (IPV)?

Intimate Partner Violence (IPV) is defined as behaviors by a current or former intimate partner that cause physical, sexual, or psychological harm. This includes forms of physical aggression, sexual coercion, psychological abuse, and controlling behaviors. The frequency and severity of IPV can vary significantly, ranging from isolated incidents to recurring and severe episodes that can last for many years.

Key Facts:

  • WHO reports that globally, about 1 in 3 women have been subjected to physical or sexual IPV in their lifetime.
  • The National Coalition Against Domestic Violence estimates that 1 in 4 women and 1 in 7 men have experienced severe physical violence by an intimate partner.
